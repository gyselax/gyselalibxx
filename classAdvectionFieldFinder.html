Detailed Description

template<class Mapping>
class AdvectionFieldFinder< Mapping >

Solve the Poisson equation and return the electric field for the coupled Vlasov equation.

The Vlasov-Poisson equations are given by

  • (1) \( \partial_t \rho - E_y \partial_x \rho + E_x \partial_y\rho = 0 \),
  • (2) \( - \Delta \phi = \rho \),
  • (3) and \( E = -\nabla \phi \).

The functions are defined on a logical domain, and the mapping from the logical domain to the physical domain is written \(\mathcal{F}\).

We here focus on equation (3). The \( \phi \) is already computed on B-splines with the given Poisson solver. Then in the AdvectionFieldRP::operator() we compute the advection field ( \(A = E \wedge e_z\)) thanks to (3) using the B-splines coefficients. Depending on the given mapping, the computation at the center point is not always well-defined so we linearize around the center point as explained in Edoardo Zoni's article (https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jcp.2019.108889).

The advection field can be computed along the logical domain axis or the physical domain axis.

1- In the first case, we compute the electric field thanks to (3) and

  • \( \nabla_{x,y} \phi(r, \theta) = (J^{-1})^{T} [\partial_r \phi, \partial_\theta \phi]^T \),
  • \( E(r, \theta) = -\nabla_{x,y} \phi(r, \theta) \),

For \( r < \varepsilon \), \((J^{-1})^{T}\) is ill-defined so we linearize \( E(r, \theta) = \left( 1 - \frac{r}{\varepsilon} \right) E(0, \theta) + \frac{r}{\varepsilon} E(\varepsilon, \theta) \),

with \( E(0, \theta) \) computed thanks to

  • \( \partial_r \phi (0, \theta_1) = \left[\partial_r x \partial_x \phi + \partial_r y \partial_y \phi \right](0, \theta_1) \),
  • \( \partial_r \phi (0, \theta_2) = \left[\partial_r x \partial_x \phi + \partial_r y \partial_y \phi \right] (0, \theta_2) \),

where \( \theta_1 \) and \( \theta_2 \) correspond to linearly independent directions.

Then the advection field along the physical domain axis is given by \(A = E \wedge e_z\).

2- In the second case, the advection field along the logical domain axis is computed with

  • \( \nabla \phi = \sum_{i,j} \partial_{x_i} f g^{ij} \sqrt{g_{jj}} \hat{e}_j\),
  • with \(g^{ij}\), the coefficients of the inverse metrix tensor,
  • \(g_{jj}\), the coefficients of the metrix tensor,
  • \(\hat{e}_j\), the normalized covariants vectors.

Then, we compute \( E = -\nabla \phi \) and \(A = E \wedge e_z\).

The equation (1) is solved thanks to advection operator (IAdvectionRP).
