Detailed Description

A class that solves a so-called "pressure-diffusive" fluid neutral model.

The equation of the model that describes the evolution of the density of neutrals can be written in dimensional units as

\(\partial_t n_n + \partial_x (n_{n,eq} u_i - D_p T_n \partial_x n_n) = S_n, \)

where \(n_n(x,t)\) is the time and space dependent neutral density, \(T_n\) is the temperature of neutrals (considered constant) and \(u_i(x)\) is the ion fluid velocity.

In the above equation the following definitions are used:

\(n_{n,eq} = \frac{n_i n_e K_r + n_n n_i K_{cx}}{n_i K_{cx} + n_e K_i}\)

and

\(D_p = \frac{1}{m_n (n_i K_{cx} + n_e K_i)}\)

where \(n_i\) (resp. \(n_e\)) is the ion (resp. electron) density and \(m_n\) stands for the mass of neutrals. The \(K_i\), \(K_r\) and \(K_{cx}\) coefficients represent the reaction rates of ionization, recombination and charge-exchange reactions.

The density source term \(S_n\) is

\(S_n = n_i n_e K_r - n_n n_e K_i.\)

The pressure-diffusive equation is normalized to the relevant normalization quantities of the geometryXVx folder:

  • densities to a reference density \(n_0\);
  • temperatures to a reference temperature \(T_0\);
  • time normalized to the electron plasma frequency \(\omega_{pe0} = \sqrt{n_0 e^2/(m_e \varepsilon_0)}\);
  • space to the Debye length \(\lambda_{D0} = \sqrt{\varepsilon_0 T_0 / (n_0 e^2)}\);
  • ion mean velocity to the ion thermal velocity \(v_{Ti0} = \sqrt{T_0/m_i}\);
  • reaction rates to a reference rate \(K_0\);
  • masses to the electron mass \(m_e\).

With these conventions the pressure-diffusive equation can be written with all quantities normalized as

\(\partial_t n_n + \partial_x (\sqrt{\frac{m_e}{m_i}}n_{n,eq} u_i - \alpha_0 D_p T_n \partial_x n_n) = \alpha_0^{-1} S_n, \)

Where \(\alpha_0\) is a normalization coefficient equal to \(\alpha_0 = \omega_{pe0}/(n_0 K_0)\). All the terms that appear in this normalized equation keep the same expression as when writing the dimensional form of the model, except that quantities involved are normalized.

The pressure-diffusive model is solved using a RK2 time integrator. Spatial derivatives are computed using splines polynomials.

Computes the expression of the time derivative of the neutral fluid moments.

The expression of the time derivative is given by the equation of the pressure-diffusive neutral model, that is to say

\(\partial_t n_n = - \partial_x (n_{n,eq} u_i - D_p T_n \partial_x n_n) + S_n\)

This function is used by the time integrator (RK2 for instance).

Parameters
[in,out]dnThe time derivative of neutral fluid moments.
[in]nThe fluid moments of the neutral species.
[in]densityThe plasma density (for ion and electrons).
[in]velocityThe plasma mean velocity (for ion and electrons).
[in]temperatureThe plasma temperature (for ion and electrons).
